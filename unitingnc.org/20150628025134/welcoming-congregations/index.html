Welcoming Congregations

Uniting NC believes that communities of faith are important sites for the welcoming of immigrants and the fostering of relationships across cultures. Many congregations and faith communities feel strongly about welcoming the foreigner and stranger among us as part of being a community of faith. But we can still do better.

Fostering welcoming congregations at the level of the individual congregation is important, but it is also important at the communal level. Uniting NC believes that through the Welcoming Congregations network of faith communities, we can make not only our own congregations stronger and more faithful, but also create more unified communities of faith across North Carolina for all peoples.

Become a Welcoming Congregation Now

What is a Welcoming Congregation?

A Welcoming Congregation is a community of faith dedicated to creating intentional spaces for dialogue and relationships between those of many different backgrounds and home cultures. These faith communities are involved in participating in one another’s lives though a mutual sharing across languages, cultures and nations in a variety of creative ways. Communities actively engaged in being a welcoming congregation gather together around food, issues of advocacy, worship and service.

How to become a Welcoming Congregation?

Many congregations in North Carolina are already engaged in building relationships and welcoming others. Uniting NC would like to recognize the communities already involved as together we work to create a stronger network of communities in North Carolina. Uniting NC is interested in partnering with congregations who would like to become welcoming congregations or congregations who would like to deepen and strengthen already existing commitments to being communities of welcome.

For congregations just starting to explore becoming a Welcoming Congregation, here are a few activities to begin with:

  • Participate in a one-time facilitated dialogue focused on immigration.
    • Uniting NC is connected with a variety of trained facilitators and resources for beginning this dialogue in your congregation. Please contact Dan Rearick (dan@unitingnc.org) to get started.
  • Participate in a one-time community service project with another congregation or group which includes immigrants or brings together diverse members of your own congregation.
    • Examples include: working on a Habitat for Humanity project, clothing drive, food collection, community garden, etc.
  • Preach a sermon on the topic of immigration.
    • See our resources on worship and immigration:
      • http://www.welcometheimmigrant.org/worship-resources
      • http://www.ncchurches.org/programs/immigrant-rights/#resources
      • http://www.ncchurches.org/programs/immigrant-rights/#sermons
      • http://www.ncchurches.org/lectionary/year-a/immigration-hospitality-proper-8/
      • http://www.ncchurches.org/lectionary/year-b/justice-for-immigrants-proper-26/
  • Include immigrants in your worship service by reading scriptures or singing in different languages.
    • Note: Many hymnals now include at least a few items in languages other than English.
  • Host a community meal that includes foods from different cultures.
  • Donate $100 or more to Uniting NC so that we can continue this work in more congregations.
  • Sign the congregational Welcoming Pledge.
    • “I support efforts to ensure that my community is one that respects and honors all the diverse people who have made it their home, regardless of culture or background.”

For congregations already in engaged in a mission of welcoming immigrants, here are a few more involved activities to participate in:

  • Organize joint community service projects like those described above on a regular ongoing basis.
  • Use multiple languages or offer prayers for immigrant concerns each week.
  • Form a “Sister Congregation” relationship with a faith community that includes people of different backgrounds, and hold at least four joint programs each year.
  • Participate in the NC Council of Churches’ “Neighbors Together” dinner-dialogue program: www.ncchurches.org/2012/11/new-pilot-program-neighbors-together.
  • Start a new immigrant-focused ministry or program, such as ESL or after-school mentoring.
  • If you share space with another congregation or members of a different culture/language, set up a leadership team with representatives from each group. Hold regular meetings to foster communication, address any concerns and plan joint programs.

Uniting NC looks forward to partnering with faith communities as we work together to make North Carolina a place of welcome for all.
